After running a design system documentation site that served its purpose and then some, I finally converted this space into something I'll actually maintain: a personal site with a blog, portfolio, and CV.

The setup is Jekyll with GitHub Actions for deployment. The design language stays — I like the card-based layout, the Inter type, and the clean structure — but all the content is new.

## What I'll write about

My work sits at the intersection of **DesignOps**, **UX strategy**, and **AI adoption**. A lot of what I do is invisible: building the scaffolding that lets design teams work at scale, shaping how organisations think about design maturity, and more recently helping teams build real AI fluency rather than surface-level tool familiarity.

I'll write about that — the messy, systems-level work of making design organisations function well, with some tangents into the tools and processes I find interesting.

## What's here

- **Blog** — irregular writing on DesignOps, UX strategy, and AI adoption
- **Portfolio** — selected work across design leadership, systems, and apps
- **CV** — full work history in a format useful to hiring managers and collaborators
- **Contact** — email and social links, and what I'm currently available for
