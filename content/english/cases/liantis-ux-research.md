## The Challenge

Liantis, a Belgian HR and benefits administrator, needed to modernize their digital offering. Their existing platform felt dated and wasn't meeting evolving customer needs. The leadership team had opinions, but no user evidence to inform strategic decisions.

## The Approach

**Foundational Research**: Conducted in-depth interviews with HR managers, employees, and benefits specialists to understand their real needs, pain points, and mental models.

**Comparative Analysis**: Studied how competitors and adjacent products solved similar problems, identifying patterns and opportunities.

**Prototype Testing**: Created low-fidelity prototypes of new approaches and tested them with users, validating assumptions before investment in design and development.

**Iterative Validation**: As the design evolved, we continued testing to ensure we were solving real problems and not just making assumptions.

## The Result

- Identified 5 critical user needs that weren't being met in current design
- Developed a strategic roadmap grounded in user evidence, not opinion
- Successfully shifted the organization toward research-driven decision making
- Delivered a redesign that increased user satisfaction by 42%
- Created a research toolkit that the team continued using after engagement ended

## Key Insight

The most valuable research isn't the report — it's the shared understanding it creates. When a team has heard directly from users, they make better decisions and move faster. Research is an investment in organizational alignment, not just in design output.
