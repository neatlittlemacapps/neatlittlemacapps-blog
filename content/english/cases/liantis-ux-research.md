## The Challenge

Liantis, a Belgian HR and benefits administrator, needed to modernize their digital offering. Their existing platform felt dated and wasn't meeting evolving customer needs. The leadership team had opinions, but no user evidence to inform strategic decisions. You know the drill.

## The Approach

Started with foundational research: in-depth interviews with HR managers, employees, and benefits specialists to understand real needs, pain points, and mental models. Then comparative analysis to see how competitors and adjacent products solved similar problems.

Created low-fidelity prototypes and tested them with users, validating assumptions before investing in design and development. As the design evolved, we kept testing to make sure we were solving real problems and not just confirming our own biases.

## The Result

Identified 5 critical user needs that weren't being met. Developed a strategic roadmap grounded in user evidence, not opinion. Shifted the organization toward research-driven decision making. Delivered a redesign that increased user satisfaction by 42%. And created a research toolkit the team continued using long after the engagement ended.

## Key Insight

The most valuable research isn't the report. It's the shared understanding it creates. When a team has heard directly from users, they make better decisions and move faster. Research is an investment in organizational alignment, not just design output.
