## The Challenge

Corilus develops healthcare software across multiple product lines. Each team had evolved its own design patterns, component libraries, and design language. The result? Inconsistency, duplicated effort, and friction between product and design teams.

## The Approach

Started with a comprehensive audit of existing design patterns across all products, identifying common needs and unique requirements. Then, rather than imposing a top-down system, facilitated workshops with designers, developers, and product leaders to define shared principles together.

Built a scalable component system that accommodated different product contexts while maintaining consistency. Created living documentation that both designers and developers could reference and contribute to.

## The Result

40% reduction in design-to-development handoff friction. Single source of truth for design patterns and components. Faster iteration cycles across all product lines. Stronger collaboration between design and engineering. And a foundation for confident scaling as the team grew.

## Key Insight

The real work of a design system isn't the components. It's the organizational alignment behind them. The system only works if everyone understands why it exists and feels ownership over it.
