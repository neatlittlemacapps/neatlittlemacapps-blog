## The Challenge

Corilus develops healthcare software solutions across multiple product lines. Each team had evolved its own design patterns, component libraries, and design language — creating inconsistency, duplicated effort, and friction between product and design teams.

## The Approach

**Audit & Synthesis**: I conducted a comprehensive audit of existing design patterns across all products, identifying common needs and unique requirements.

**Collaborative Design**: Rather than imposing a top-down system, I facilitated workshops with designers, developers, and product leaders to define shared principles.

**Component Architecture**: Built a scalable component system that accommodated different product contexts while maintaining consistency.

**Documentation First**: Created living documentation that designers and developers could both reference and contribute to.

## The Result

- 40% reduction in design-to-development handoff friction
- Single source of truth for design patterns and components
- Faster iteration cycles across all product lines
- Stronger collaboration between design and engineering
- Foundation for confident scaling as the team grew

## Key Insight

The real work of a design system isn't the components — it's the organizational alignment behind them. The system only works if everyone understands why it exists and feels ownership over it.
