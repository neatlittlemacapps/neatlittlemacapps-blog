## The Challenge

Infinitas Learning was growing fast but had no formal design operations. Designers scattered across initiatives, working in silos. Inconsistent quality, unclear handoff, no shared tooling or documentation. And burnout from lack of clarity and process.

## The Approach

Started by spending a month understanding how work actually flowed, where friction lived, and what processes were basically made up on the spot.

Then: defined design workflows for research, concepting, validation, and handoff (flexible, not dogmatic). Implemented Figma as the single source of truth with component libraries and documentation systems. Created roles and responsibilities that were clear yet allowed growth. Set up regular retrospectives so processes improved based on the team's own feedback.

## The Result

Grew from 2 to 15+ designers in three years without losing culture. Reduced project turnaround time by 35%. Established documented processes that new team members could pick up quickly. Created a design system that served as a foundation for product consistency. And built a team that felt enabled rather than constrained by process.

## Key Insight

DesignOps isn't about process for its own sake. It's about removing friction so designers can focus on solving hard problems. The best ops are invisible... the team doesn't think about process because it just works.
