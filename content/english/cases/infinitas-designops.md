## The Challenge

Infinitas Learning was growing rapidly but lacked formal design operations. With designers scattered across different initiatives and working in silos, we faced inconsistent design quality, unclear handoff processes, no shared tooling or documentation, and burnout from lack of clarity and process.

## The Approach

**Audit & Assessment**: I spent the first month understanding how work actually flowed — where friction existed and what processes were ad-hoc.

**Core Process Design**: Defined design workflows for research, concepting, validation, and handoff. Not dogmatic — designed to be flexible but intentional.

**Tool Strategy**: Implemented Figma as the single source of truth, established component libraries, and set up documentation systems.

**Team Development**: Created roles and responsibilities that were clear yet flexible, allowing growth and specialization.

**Continuous Improvement**: Established regular retrospectives to improve processes based on team feedback.

## The Result

- Grew from 2 to 15+ designers in three years without losing culture
- Reduced project turnaround time by 35%
- Established documented processes that new team members could quickly learn
- Created a design system that served as a foundation for product consistency
- Built a team that felt enabled rather than constrained by process

## Key Insight

DesignOps isn't about process for its own sake. It's about removing friction so designers can focus on solving hard problems. The best ops are invisible — the team doesn't think about process because it works.
